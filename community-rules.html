Community rules

These rules keep Unmuted useful and as safe as possible for people sharing real experiences about work and society. By using Unmuted, you agree to follow them.

What to share

  • Your experience. Write in the first person about what you saw, felt, or went through. Do not impersonate someone else or invent stories.
  • Protect people’s privacy. Do not post private contact details, home addresses, ID numbers, or other information that could identify or endanger someone. Use roles (“a supervisor”) when names are not essential.
  • Be careful with accusations. Share what happened to you without turning the platform into a place for naming-and-shaming campaigns. Facts matter; reckless false claims about identifiable people are not allowed.

What not to do

  • No harassment, threats, or hate. That includes slurs, dehumanizing language, and encouraging violence or self-harm.
  • No illegal or non-consensual material. Do not upload or describe sexual content involving minors, or share intimate images or leaks without consent.
  • No doxxing or coordinated attacks. Do not organize brigades, mass reporting as harassment, or attempts to find someone’s private information.
  • No spam or scams. Unmuted is not for ads, MLMs, or misleading links.

How we enforce

  • Moderation. We may remove content, limit reach, or suspend accounts that break these rules or put people at risk.
  • Reports. Use reporting tools when something feels unsafe or off. Good-faith reports help everyone.
  • Appeals. If you believe we made a mistake, you can appeal through the process we publish in product or support channels.

Important limits

  • Unmuted is not a court or a lawyer. What you read here is personal experience and discussion, not legal, medical, or professional advice.
  • Safety is not guaranteed. We provide tools and rules to reduce risk, but we cannot promise anonymity or protection from every harm. Post only what you can sustain sharing.
Remember: If you or someone else is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. Unmuted cannot intervene in emergencies.

Last updated: April 2026. We may update these rules; continued use means you accept the current version.
